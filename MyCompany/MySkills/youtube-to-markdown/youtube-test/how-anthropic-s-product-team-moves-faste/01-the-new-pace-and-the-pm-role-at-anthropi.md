# The New Pace and the PM Role at Anthropic

> Anthropic's product timelines have collapsed from 6 months to 1 day, and most PM candidates Cat interviews are still optimizing for the old world.

## Key Points
- Feature timelines at Anthropic have moved from **6 months → 1 month → sometimes 1 day**.
- Cat is **Head of Product for Cloud Code and Co-work**; Boris (creator of Claude Code) is the team's tech lead and longer-horizon product visionary.
- Their split is "**80% mind-meld, 20% specialization**": Boris owns the 3–6 month vision, Cat owns the path-from-today and the cross-functional pipeline (marketing, sales, finance, capacity).
- Cat is interviewing **hundreds of PMs** and seeing the same wrong instinct: trying to align multi-quarter roadmaps with partner teams instead of compressing idea-to-user time.
- The new core PM job is **"how do I get this idea into users' hands by end of week?"** plus **defining the few tasks that must work out-of-the-box** for a feature.
- AI-native PMs are evaluated on velocity-enabling skills, not coordination skills.

## Details

**Lenny opens with three teaser quotes from Cat** that frame the whole episode: "It's very hard to be the right amount of AGI-pilled"; "We want to remove every single barrier to shipping things"; and "It comes back to product taste — as code becomes much cheaper to write, the thing that becomes more valuable is deciding what to write." Each is unpacked later in the conversation.

**Cat's relationship with Boris.** Boris is described as the most popular guest the podcast has ever had and the person who created Claude Code. Cat says Boris "is great at setting like this is what the product needs to be in 3 months, 6 months from now — like what the AGI-pilled version of the product is." Her job is to draw the line from today's product to that vision and to make sure the cross-functional org doesn't become a blocker. The "80% mind-meld" framing matters: it lets either of them speak for the team, which is a precondition for high-velocity decision-making.

**Why interviewing PMs has become alarming.** Pre-AI tech shifts were slow enough that PMs could plan 6–12 months out and spend most of their time aligning partner teams. Cat says today the math has flipped: "code at that time was very expensive to make. Now with AI and how much that has accelerated engineering and how quickly the model capabilities are improving, the timelines for a lot of our product features have gone down from 6 months to one month and sometimes to one week or even one day."

The candidates she interviews still talk in multi-quarter roadmaps. Her diagnosis: the highest-leverage skill is now **shortening idea-to-user latency** and **scoping the must-work tasks** — not coordinating across organizations.
